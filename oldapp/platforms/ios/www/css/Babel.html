About this app: I teach family medicine at the University of Ottawa, in Canada. Our patients speak French and English, and many other languages.

All people love to be spoken to in their own language, especially when they are ill. I have found the shortest possible questionnaire needed to ask about someone's health. There are just 13 expressions:
- Hello / Goodbye
- What is the problem?
- Yes, no
- Fever
- Pain
- Here (a part of the body)
- Respiration
- Urine
- Stool
- Diarrhea
- Vomit
- Blood
- Pregnant

Of course, there will be smiling, hand waving and pointing as well.

Doctor of Babel presents these 13 expressions in 14 different languages, in written and audio form. I chose these languages because there are native speakers of each language in our clinic.

I have used this list when caring for the Ngobe people in the jungles of Panama, and for the Cree in northern QueÌbec. I have also used these expressions when caring for refugees in Ottawa.

To find out more about eachlanguage, touch the > sign in the upper right corner of the page.

There are fascinating discoveries to be made with this app. For example, 'Dard' is the word for 'Pain' in Dari, Pashtu and Farsi.
